OSI Model

The OSI model is a conceptual standard for visualizing the 7 layers computer networking communication.
Unlike Dante's 7 layers, network administrators can easily identify and troubleshoot network issues by followiing the standard from bottom to top.



Image Credit: guru99


The OSI model helps network technicians and administrators to visualize the flow of traffic across a netowrk, identify where problems may arise, and provides a framework for categorizing different components of a network. The OSI model can be further divided into three sections, as shown in the image above. Layers 5-7 are known as the software layes. Layer 4 is the layer involved in creating and disconnecting sessions between two machines on a network. The hardware that creates a network can be placed in layes 1-3. Not everything to do with networking will always fit neatly into a specific layer. This is meant to be used as a general framework to aid in visualization and troubleshooting.
